Where does free EV charging data come from?

WattsNear uses the U.S. Department of Energy Alternative Fuels Station Locator, maintained by NREL (National Renewable Energy Laboratory). It's the most comprehensive public dataset of EV charging infrastructure in the country, updated daily.

Free charging is labeled in the dataset when station operators report $0 pricing. That includes destination chargers at hotels and retailers, workplace chargers, and municipal parking chargers subsidized by local governments.

Because the data comes from a government database — not user reports or station-operator marketing — it's more reliable than crowd-sourced alternatives.

Common questions about free EV charging

Are there actually free EV chargers near me?
Likely yes. Many retailers (Whole Foods, IKEA, some Walmarts), hotels, and municipal parking structures offer free Level 2 charging to attract customers. Download WattsNear to see what's around you right now.
What level are most free EV chargers?
Most free chargers are Level 2 (AC, ~10–30 miles of range per hour). Free DC fast chargers exist but are rare — they're usually promotional and time-limited.
Do I need a membership card or app for free chargers?
Some free chargers require a free network account (e.g., ChargePoint). Others are fully open — just plug in. WattsNear shows network information for each station so you know what to expect.
